Based on what you've told us You get help with NHS costs

Because you get income-related Employment and Support Allowane (ESA)

You get free:

  • ✅ NHS prescriptions
  • ✅ NHS dental check-ups and treatment
  • ✅ sight tests
  • ✅ NHS wigs and fabric supports

You get some money off:

  • ✅ new glasses and contact lenses
  • ✅ repairing or replacing your glasses and contact lenses
  • ✅ travel for NHS treatment

Any children under 20 included on your Universal Credit claim are also entitled to full help with NHS costs.

If you are pregnant or have children

  • As part of the Healthy Start scheme, if you're at least ten weeks pregnant or have a child under the age of four, you may be entitled to:

  • ✅ £3.10 a week to spend on foods such as cow’s milk, baby formula, fresh or frozen fruit and vegetables
  • ✅ free vitamins

    • Find out more about who qualifies for Healthy Start

    How to get your help

    You don't have to do anything more, although you may have to show proof you get Universal Credit to your pharmacist, optician or dentist.

    You can use your latest Universal Credit award notice as proof.

    If you have lost yours contact the Jobcentre Plus Helpline.

    Help with travel for NHS treatment

    Tell the hospital (or the place where you're getting treatment) that you want to claim back the cost of your journey. They'll tell you what you need to do.

    You may need to show proof:

    • of your travel costs, for example, bus tickets
    • that you're entitled to help

    Warning Your take-home pay changes each assessment period. To avoid any possible penalty charge, check before you claim.
