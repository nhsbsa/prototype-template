Based on what you've told us You get help with NHS costs

Because you get Income Support:

You get free:

  • NHS prescriptions
  • NHS dental check-ups and treatment
  • sight tests
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports

You get money off:

  • new glasses or contact lenses
  • repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • travel for NHS treatment

Any children under 20 included on your Income Support claim are also entitled to the same help.

If you are pregnant or have children

  • As part of the Healthy Start scheme, if you're at least than ten weeks pregnant or have a child under the age of four, you may be entitled to:
  • £3.10 a week to spend on foods such as cow’s milk, baby formula, fresh or frozen fruit and vegetables
  • free vitamins

    • Find out more about who qualifies for Healthy Start

    How to get your help

    In most cases, you don't have to do anything more, although you may have to show proof you get Income Support to your pharmacist, optician or dentist.

    You can use your latest benefit letter or award notice as proof.
    If you have lost yours contact the Jobcentre Plus Helpline.

    Help with travel for NHS treatment

    Tell the hospital (or the place where you’re getting treatment) that you want to claim back the cost of your journey. They’ll tell you what you need to do.

    You may need to show proof:

    • of your travel costs, for example, bus tickets
    • that you’re entitled to help

    Warning It's important you know what benefit you get - you can get a fine if you claim help with NHS costs and you're not entitled.
